Exploring the Interplay Between Global Population Growth and State-wise Tourism Rates in Malaysia

Malaysia's tourism landscape is diverse, with each state offering unique attractions. From the bustling city life of Kuala Lumpur to the pristine beaches of Langkawi and the cultural richness of Penang, tourism rates vary significantly across regions. Understanding these state-wise dynamics helps identify areas for development and investment, ensuring that tourism can contribute to local economies while preserving cultural and natural resources.
The global population has seen unprecedented growth from 1950 to 2020, expanding from approximately 2.5 billion to nearly 7.8 billion. This surge has profound implications for resource management, urbanization, and economic development worldwide. In the context of Malaysia, this trend influences tourism patterns, as a larger global population increases the potential for travel and cultural exchange, impacting the country's tourism strategy and infrastructure planning.
